Para-athelics

Para-athletics is the largest discipline in the Paralympic program and comprises several modalities: track events, with distances ranging from 100 to 10,000 meters; competitions, with jumps and throws; and the marathon, which is held in the streets. Some athletes compete in wheelchairs, with prostheses or with the help of a non-visually impaired guide.

In addition to being the modality with the largest number of athletes and events in the Paralympic Games, athletics is also the one with the largest number of disability classes. Each participant has a two-digit category, the first of which indicates the type of functional disability he or she has. The second digit is the degree of impairment:



  • Classes 11-13: blind and visually impaired athletes.
  • Class 20: athletes with intellectual disabilities.
  • Classes 31-64: people with physical disabilities or cerebral palsy.
  • Classes 31-38: participants with coordination problems, some of them in wheelchairs (31-34).
  • Classes 40-41: athletes with achondroplasia.
  • Classes 42-44: athletes with lower extremity impairment competing without prosthesis.
  • Classes 45-47: athletes with upper extremity involvement.
  • Classes 51-57: include wheelchair runners or throwers competing in a seated position.
  • Classes 61-64: athletes with lower extremity impairment competing with prostheses.

T or F in front of the number indicates whether the athlete competes in track events, such as running and jumping, or field events, throwing events.
