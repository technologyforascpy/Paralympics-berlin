The energy sources

Thermal power stations

Classic thermoelectric power plants use the heat that comes from fossil fuels susch as coal,natural gas and petroleum derivatives as an energy sources. The fuels is burned in a boiler, generating heat energy that is used that circulates through a serie of pipes. This water vapor is what drives the blades of the steam turbine, and thus converts heat energy into mechanical energy, wich will then lead to the generation of electrical energy in the alternator.

Advantage

  • Its cheap
  • Energy sources easy to find

    • Disadvantage

  • Energy are used that, when burned, give rise to greenhouse gases.
  • Many of the sources are not renewable

Hydroelectric sources

A hydroelectric plants is a power plant that use water to generate electricity. Generate electricity. The falling water flows through turbine hydraulic causes its axis to rotate and convert the potential energy of falling water into mechanical energy. In turn, the turbine shaft is connected to the alternator, therefore it is called an alternator and transmits its rotational mechanical energy, converted into electricity.

Advantage

  • It is a clean energy source without waste easy to store Besides regulate the flow of the river

    • Disadvantage

    • Building hydroelectric power plants is expensive
    • large power lines are needed

    Wind power plants

     Wind power plants are generators of electricity activated by the action of the wind. The wind moves the propeller and through a mechanical system of gears rotates the rotor of a generator, which produces the electric current.

    Advantage

    • It is an inexhaustible source of energy, once the installation is done, free and does not pollute.

    Disadvantage

    • It is an intermittent power source

    Photovoltaic

    Photovoltaic power plants are renewable and clean energy plants that take advantage of solar radiation to generate electricity. It is based on the so-called photoelectric effect, by which certain materials are able to absorb photons and release electrons, thus producing an electric current.This uses semiconductor devices called photovoltaic cells or cells, which can be made of monocrystalline, polycrystalline or amorphous silicon or other thin-film semiconductor materials. Monocrystalline silicon is obtained from a single crystal of pure silicon.

    Advantages

    • It is an inexhaustible source of energy and does not pollute

    Disadvantage

    • It is an intermittent power source and they have low performance.

    Nuclear energy and power plants

    Nuclear energy is the energy contained in the nucleus of an atom. Atoms are the smallest smallest particles into which a chemical element can be split while maintaining its properties. In the nucleus of each atom there are two types of particles (neutrons and protons) that are held together. together. Nuclear energy is the energy that holds neutrons and protons together.

    Nuclear energy can be used to produce electricity. But first the energy must be released.

    When the nuclear reaction occurs, the atoms experience a slight loss of mass. This lost mass is converted into a large amount of heat energy and radiation, as Albert Einstein discovered with his famous equation E=mc².

    The heat energy produced is used to produce steam and generate electricity with a turbine, with a generator.

    Advantage

    • it is a safe and clean alternative. Many of its defenders, including important organizations and some doctrinaires, consider it an excellent option for guaranteeing a large percentage of the electricity supply, generating employment, and practically not polluting; they maintain that it does not generate Carbon Dioxide (CO2), and that discharges from nuclear power plants abroad are minimal.

      • Disadvantage

      • High costs (they argue that it is very expensive to build and operate a nuclear power plant, as well as to dismantle it at the end of its useful life);
      • Threat to human health and ecosystems (its risks and impacts extend from uranium mining, the manufacture of nuclear fuels, generation of radioactive waste, transport and storage of these)
      • Possibility of a nuclear accident
      • Possibility of diversion of nuclear material for nuclear weapons (nuclear proliferation)

      Waste and treatment

      • Waste of low and medium radioactive activity: Due to its characteristics, it can be temporarily stored, treated, conditioned and stored definitively in the facilities and with a half-life of less than 30 years.
      • High activity residues (RAAs) (contain long-lived, alpha-emitting radionuclides that halve their activity in more than 30 years). They are basically made up of spent nuclear fuel.
      • Reduction of the volume of waste. (Evaporation, filtration, centrifugation, decontamination)
      • Removal of certain radionuclides. (Precipitation, degassing)

      environment

      • Beyond certain thresholds, radiation can affect the functioning of organs and tissues, and produce acute effects such as skin redness, hair loss, radiation burns, or acute irradiation syndrome. These effects are more intense with higher doses and higher dose rates.
      • radioactive materials released into the environment can cause pollution of air, water, surfaces, soils, plants, buildings, people or animals. A contaminated person has radioactive materials in or inside their body.

      Wind energy

      • Through a wind turbine that transforms the kinetic energy of the air currents into electrical energy. The extraction process is carried out mainly thanks to the rotor, which transforms kinetic energy into mechanical energy, and the generator, which transforms this mechanical energy into electrical energy. We are talking about renewable energy.
      • Advantages: It is an inexhaustible source of energy and, once the installation is done, free. In addition, it does not pollute: since there is no combustion, it does not produce acid rain, it does not contribute to the increase of the greenhouse effect, it does not destroy the ozone layer and does not generate waste.
      • Disadvantages: It is an intermittent power source, as it depends on the regularity of the winds. In addition, wind turbines are large and expensive.

      Biomass energy

      Biomass is organic matter used as an energy source. By its broad definition, biomass covers a wide set of organic materials that is characterized by its heterogeneity, both by its origin and by its nature.

      Its transformations:The valuation of biomass can be done through four basic processes by which it can be transformed into heat and electricity: combustion, pyrolysis, gasification and anaerobic digestion. Combustion. Biomass is subjected to high temperatures with excess oxygen.

      Hydropower

      Hydraulic energy is produced by water retained in reservoirs or swamps at high altitude (which has gravitational potential energy). If at any given time it is dropped to a lower level, this energy is converted into kinetic energy and, subsequently, into electrical energy in the hydroelectric plant thanks to a generator.

      Advantage

      • It is a clean energy source, without waste and easy to store. In addition, the water stored in reservoirs located in high places allows to regulate the flow of the river.

      Disadvantages

      • The construction of hydroelectric power plants is expensive and large power lines are needed. In addition, the reservoirs produce losses of productive soil and terrestrial fauna due to the flooding of the land destined for them. They also cause the flow of rivers and streams under the dam to decrease and alter water quality.

      Natural gas

      It is a mixture of gases, in which a larger portion of methane is provided. Natural gas is one of the cleanest energy sources, since it is the one that contains less carbon dioxide being non-renewable and the one that throws lower emissions into the atmosphere.

      Transformation from natural gas to electricity: Natural gas is burned in a gas turbine burner that drives a generator to produce electricity. The hot exhaust gases coming from the gas turbine are sent to a heat recovery steam generator to generate subcritical or supercritical steam.

      Advantage

      • It is a safe energy with very little environmental impact
      • It has a high calorific value
      • It is an energy with a better price
      • No storage space required

      Disadvantage

      • It is not renewable like aerothermal, although it is the least polluting gas
      • Produces greenhouse gases
      • Depends on third countries
      • Misuse can lead to a fire

      Natural gas produces greenhouse gases such as carbon dioxide or methane that favor global warming. Carbon dioxide, by increasing its presence in the atmosphere, is producing global warming, better known as climate change, which produces heat waves and floods. Methane, on the other hand, is less its presence, but it does contribute when it comes to trapping the heat of these gases.

      oil

      Petroleum is an oily liquid, less dense than water, dark in colour and with a strong odour, which is made up of a mixture of hydrocarbons and whose main characteristic is that it can burn easily. Oil is transformed into energy through thermal combustion plants, which have steam turbines to convert the thermal energy of combustion into mechanical energy, thus functioning as an electrical generator.

      Advantage

      • It has a lot of energy and efficient power
      • Very abundant source for the amount of reserves
      • Its extraction is very simple
      • It is an easy to store product
      • Economic bost

      Disadvantage

      • Emits polluting gases into the atmosphere
      • Generates products that become non-recyclable garbage
      • Changing prices [a lot of instability]
      • It is a non-renewable resource

      Polluting gases emitted

      Oil emits oxides of sulfur and nitrogen, heavy metals, benzene, toluene, polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons, are within the huge range of pollutants emitted.

      The emitted oxides of sulfur and nitrogen pollute by the gases it expels, these oxides can be converted into acids that can lead to acid rains in case of precipitation.

      Heavy metals contaminate the soil and accumulate in plants and organic tissues. The danger is that most of it is not chemically or biologically degradable. Once emitted, they can stay in the atmosphere for hundreds of years.

      Polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons are derived from oil and coal that are two elements that already pollute, because this is being derived worse. Its consequences on the environment are harmful, as it has toxic, mutagenic or carcinogenic properties.

      Disadvantage

      • Atmospheric emission of greenhouse gases (CO2, SO2, NO and CO)
      • Waste, emission and contamination of soil and water in the extraction process
      • Non-renewable

      Coal produces carbon dioxide, which enters the atmosphere through the burning of fossil fuels and also as a result of certain chemical reactions, this is one of the main gases that produces that pollutes the atmosphere. You could say that carbon dioxide is the main cause of climate change and underground mining, methane gas, which is a potent greenhouse gas.
